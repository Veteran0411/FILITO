Limits, Fits and Tolerances

Introduction to Limits and Fits

Limits and fits are a set of rules regarding the dimensions and tolerances of mating machined parts. They are used to ensure that the parts fit together properly and function as intended. .

Types of Fits

There are three main types of fits: clearance fits, transition fits, and interference fits.

Clearance Fits

Clearance fits have a small amount of clearance between the shaft and the hole. This allows the parts to move freely relative to each other. Clearance fits are used in applications where the parts need to be able to slide or rotate easily.

Transition Fits

Transition fits have a small amount of interference between the shaft and the hole. This means that the parts will be slightly tight when assembled. Transition fits are used in applications where the parts need to be held together securely, but not so tightly that they cannot be disassembled.

Interference Fits

Interference fits have a large amount of interference between the shaft and the hole. This means that the parts will be very tight when assembled. Interference fits are used in applications where the parts need to be held together very securely, such as in bearings and gears.

What is Tolerance ?

Permissible variation in the size (dimension) of a component to suit Functional Requirements

Tolerance Charts

Tolerance charts are used to select the correct fit for a particular application. The charts list the different types of fits and the tolerances for each.
